Classic Margherita Pizza

Serves: 4 | Prep time: 90 min | Cook time: 12 min

INGREDIENTS

Dough:
- 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
- 7g (1 packet) instant yeast
- 10g (2 tsp) salt
- 5g (1 tsp) sugar
- 325ml (1⅓ cups) warm water (40°C / 105°F)
- 15ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

Tomato Sauce:
- 400g can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Salt and black pepper to taste

Toppings:
- 250g fresh mozzarella, torn into pieces
- Fresh basil leaves
- Extra-virgin olive oil for drizzling
- Flaky sea salt

INSTRUCTIONS

1. Make the dough: Combine flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Add warm water and olive oil.
   Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.

2. First rise: Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a
   warm spot for 1 hour until doubled in size.

3. Make the sauce: Gently sauté garlic in olive oil for 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes,
   oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes. Let cool.

4. Preheat oven to its maximum temperature (ideally 250°C / 480°F) with a baking stone
   or heavy baking sheet inside.

5. Divide dough into 2 balls. On a floured surface, stretch each ball into a thin 30cm
   (12-inch) round. Do not use a rolling pin — stretch gently with your hands.

6. Assemble: Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the dough. Distribute mozzarella
   evenly. Do not overload.

7. Bake for 10–12 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbling with
   light brown spots.

8. Finish: Top with fresh basil, a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil, and flaky sea salt.
   Serve immediately.

TIPS
- Cold-ferment the dough in the fridge overnight for more flavour.
- Use a pizza stone for the crispiest crust.
- Less is more with toppings — the classic has only three.
